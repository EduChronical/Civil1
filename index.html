Maybelline Cream Allegedly Causes Permanent Facial Disfigurement; Consumer Alleges Life Ruined, Firm Seeks More Details

Manoj claims severe adverse reaction to cosmetic product left him disfigured; says proof already submitted, calls response insensitive and negligent

Investigation by India Today Consumer Desk
New Delhi | Updated: 26 February 2026, 20:15 IST

New Delhi: A consumer has made a grave allegation against beauty major Maybelline New York, claiming that a defective chemical cream caused severe, permanent facial disfigurement and destroyed his personal and professional life. The complainant, identified as Manoj, has stated that he already submitted photographic evidence of the damage, but the company’s consumer care team has replied asking for the same details again, prompting outrage and accusations of delay tactics.

In an email response dated 22 January 2026, Maybelline’s consumer care division asked Manoj to share details to enable a review by medical and technical experts. Manoj has vehemently denied any failure to submit evidence and accused the brand of ignoring his trauma.

“They have damaged my face, disfigured me permanently, and destroyed my whole life. I already sent the images. They are negligent and avoiding accountability.” — Manoj, Complainant

Manoj told India Today that he suffered an extreme adverse reaction to the cream, leading to permanent scarring, discoloration, and visible disfigurement. He said the harm has caused severe mental distress, social isolation, and loss of livelihood, amounting to a complete upheaval of his life.

The consumer has slammed the company’s response as insensitive, unethical, and designed to evade liability. He has demanded immediate acknowledgment, full liability, urgent medical support, and compensation for the irreversible harm caused by the allegedly defective product.

India Today has learned:
• Complainant claims photographic proof submitted earlier
• Firm asks for same details in official reply
• Alleged permanent facial disfigurement and life disruption
• Consumer threatens regulatory & legal action

Maybelline New York is yet to respond to India Today’s request for comment on the allegations, the status of the complaint, and the review process. The company has not clarified whether the evidence was received, lost, or not processed.

Consumer rights activists say such delays in addressing severe adverse reactions from cosmetics violate fair trade practices and consumer protection norms. Experts warn that cosmetic products causing permanent physical harm fall under strict liability and demand prompt redressal.

Manoj has warned he will escalate the matter to consumer courts, drug and cosmetic regulatory authorities, and launch a public campaign to warn other users if the company continues to delay or ignore his grievance.

India Today will closely monitor the case and provide updates as the story develops.
